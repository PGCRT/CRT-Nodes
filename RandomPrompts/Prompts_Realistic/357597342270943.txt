**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A young woman with long, straight black hair, a small rose tattoo on her left upper arm, kneeling on a cluster of rough, brownish-gray stones in a natural setting. Her hands rest lightly on her thighs, fingers slightly curled, as if she’s about to lift something or simply balancing. Her gaze is direct, almost candid, with a subtle, natural expression that suggests she’s unaware of the camera.

- **Clothing & Appearance**  
  She wears a simple, light‑colored, off‑the‑shoulder top that drapes loosely over her shoulders, revealing a hint of her collarbone and the curve of her neck. The fabric is a soft, natural fiber—likely cotton or linen—with a slight wrinkling from the wind or movement. Her skin is tanned, with a warm, sun‑kissed tone, and a faint, natural moisture on her chest that hints at recent water or dew.

- **Environment**  
  The background consists of a dense patch of spiky, green agave plants behind her, their broad leaves creating a textured, almost chaotic backdrop. The ground is covered in a mix of smooth, rounded rocks and small pebbles, with a few dried reeds scattered about. The scene is set in a sunlit meadow at late afternoon; the light is golden‑warm, coming from the west, casting long, soft shadows that stretch toward the north.

- **Time of Day & Weather**  
  Late afternoon (around 5 pm), with the sun low but not too low, creating a gentle overexposure on the subject’s face and a slight warmth in the air. The weather is mild, with a light breeze that ruffles her hair and the leaves. A few clouds drift lazily across the sky, adding depth to the scene.

- **Lens & Technical**  
  Use a wide‑angle lens (24 mm or 35 mm) with a short focal length to capture a broad view of the subject and environment. Shoot in raw mode, no auto‑HDR or auto‑beauty enhancements; keep the image unprocessed. Aim for a slight overexposure on the subject’s face to give it a natural, candid feel. Keep everything in focus—no bokeh, no depth‑of‑field blur—so the background remains sharp.

- **Capture Style**  
  Capture a candid, unposed moment; the subject should appear mid‑action or slightly unaware of the camera. Encourage imperfections: a slight tilt in the frame, an off‑center composition, and a partial crop that doesn’t follow the rule‑of‑thirds. The framing should feel accidental, as if the photographer was simply capturing a fleeting moment.

- **Lighting & Composition**  
  Zero studio lighting; rely entirely on natural light. Use a wide lens to keep the entire scene—subject, rocks, plants, and background—in focus. Keep the composition spontaneous: the subject is slightly off‑center, the hands are not perfectly aligned, and the background isn’t symmetrical. The final image should feel like a genuine, raw snapshot of a candid moment in nature.
