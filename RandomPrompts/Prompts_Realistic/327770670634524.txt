**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework – “Summer Afternoon on the Balcony”**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A woman in a light, flowing summer dress, captured mid‑turn as she adjusts her scarf. Her face is partially turned toward the camera, a candid, unposed expression with a faint smile. The shot focuses on her upper body and one arm, with her left hand gripping the edge of a wooden balcony railing.  

- **Motion & Focus**  
  Slight motion blur at the edges from handheld shake; the center of the frame (the woman’s torso) is crisp while the periphery has a soft, natural blur. No portrait‑mode depth‑of‑field blur – everything from foreground to background stays in focus.  

- **Lighting & Exposure**  
  Auto‑HDR off. The scene is lit by warm, mid‑afternoon sunlight coming from the left, creating a gentle overexposure on the woman’s shoulder and a subtle highlight on the railing. A slight warmth tint keeps the look raw, unprocessed.  

- **Lens & Technical**  
  24 mm wide‑angle lens (short focal length). No bokeh – the background (a brick wall, a small garden, a distant tree) is fully sharp. The composition is accidental: the frame is slightly tilted, off‑center, and the crop cuts off part of the woman’s head and the railing, giving a spontaneous feel.  

- **Environment**  
  A wooden balcony in an old apartment building, mid‑summer afternoon. The time of day is late‑afternoon (around 4 pm), with warm, golden light and a faint haze from the sun. A small garden with a few green plants is visible beyond the railing; a brick wall runs along the back of the balcony. The weather is clear, with a light breeze.  

- **Details & Imperfections**  
  Slight tilt of the camera, a partial crop that cuts off the top of the woman’s head and the lower part of the railing. A few stray hairs cling to her ear, a small smudge on her sleeve, and a faint dust particle on the railing – all adding realism. The woman’s scarf is slightly askew, her left hand is gripping the rail as if she just finished tying it.
