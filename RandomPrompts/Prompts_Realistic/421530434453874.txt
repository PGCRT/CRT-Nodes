**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A young woman with long, wavy brown hair tied back with a thin white headband, wearing an off‑the‑shoulder white ribbed top and matching high‑waisted shorts. She stands mid‑step on a sunlit forest trail, left hand on hip, right arm relaxed by her side, body angled slightly toward the camera. Her face is turned to the viewer with a subtle, natural smile; slight motion blur at the edges conveys movement.

- **Lighting & Exposure**  
  Ambient daylight from a high‑angle, late‑afternoon sun creates a soft overexposure on the subject’s front while the background remains fully sharp. No auto‑HDR or beauty filters—raw, unprocessed look with a slight warm tint. The exposure is slightly uneven, giving an authentic handheld feel.

- **Environment**  
  Location: A pine‑forest trail in a mountain meadow, with tall slender tree trunks lining a dirt path. Small pink wildflowers dot the grass on either side, and a large, weathered stone lies near the trail’s bend. The scene is bathed in golden hour light, with gentle shadows and a slight haze from the atmosphere.

- **Time of Day & Weather**  
  Late afternoon (around 5 pm), clear sky with a thin veil of clouds. Ambient temperature is mild, the wind is light, creating a natural “mid‑action” vibe.

- **Lens & Technical Settings**  
  - Focal length: 24 mm (wide‑angle, short focal length).  
  - Aperture: f/5.6 (handheld, everything in focus).  
  - Shutter speed: 1/125 s (slight motion blur).  
  - ISO: 400 (natural grain).  
  - No bokeh; background fully sharp.  
  - No portrait‑mode subject isolation—everything is in frame.

- **Composition & Framing**  
  Candid, unposed, spontaneous framing with a slight tilt and off‑center crop. The composition does not follow the rule of thirds; the subject’s head is slightly above the center line, and the trail leads diagonally across the frame. The shot feels “mid‑action” as if she just stepped forward.

- **Post‑Processing**  
  Zero filters, zero color grading, zero post‑processing aesthetic. Slight overexposure on the subject, natural contrast, raw look.
