**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A young woman with wet, tousled hair laughing mid‑conversation, seated in an office chair, wearing a white tank top and lavender leggings. Her left arm is extended toward the camera, creating a diagonal line that gives a dynamic, spontaneous feel. The shot should capture her expression and the slight tilt of her head as if she’s reacting to something said by the person next to her (only the back of that person’s head is visible).

- **Motion & Focus**  
  Slight motion blur on the subject’s face and extended arm to convey handheld instability. Everything in the frame—subject, background, and foreground—should be fully sharp, no portrait‑mode blur. Use a wide‑angle lens (20–35 mm) with a short focal length to keep the entire scene, including the office environment, in view.

- **Lighting & Exposure**  
  Natural daylight from a north‑west window; slight overexposure on the subject’s face to give a raw, unprocessed look. No auto‑HDR or beauty filters—just a straightforward exposure that keeps the whole frame bright but unedited. The lighting should be diffused, with no harsh shadows.

- **Environment**  
  Office setting: a desk with a computer monitor, a stack of papers, a pen holder, and a small plant in the background. A glass partition is visible behind the woman, reflecting the ceiling lights. The time of day is mid‑afternoon (around 3 pm), with warm natural light. Weather is clear, with a slight breeze that keeps the hair slightly moving.

- **Composition & Framing**  
  No rule‑of‑thirds intentionality—frame the scene as if you’re handheld, off‑center, and slightly tilted. The composition should feel accidental: the woman’s head is not perfectly centered; her left arm extends beyond the frame’s right edge. Keep the background fully sharp, no bokeh.

- **Technical Settings**  
  - Lens: 35 mm wide‑angle, f/4.0, ISO 400  
  - Shutter speed: 1/60 s (to allow slight blur)  
  - Aperture: f/4 (for sharp background)  
  - Focus: manual or autofocus on the subject’s face  
  - No portrait‑mode depth‑of‑field blur—everything in focus.

- **Style Notes**  
  Capture a candid, unposed moment that feels spontaneous and real. The image should look like a genuine snapshot taken by a friend in an office, with natural imperfections: slight tilt, off‑center framing, partial crop. Avoid any post‑processing or color grading—just raw, in‑camera aesthetics.

**Prompt Summary**  
“Candid photo of a laughing woman in a white tank top and lavender leggings, seated in an office chair, extending her left arm toward the camera, captured with a 35 mm wide‑angle lens at f/4 and 1/60 s shutter speed. Slight motion blur on the subject, full sharpness throughout the frame, natural daylight from a north‑west window, mid‑afternoon. Office background includes a desk, computer monitor, papers, pen holder, and a plant; glass partition reflects ceiling lights. Frame is off‑center and slightly tilted, no rule‑of‑thirds, raw, unprocessed look.”
