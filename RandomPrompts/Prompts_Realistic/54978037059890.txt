**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A young woman with long, sun‑kissed hair standing barefoot on a pine‑covered forest floor. She wears a muted brown crop top and light denim shorts, holding a thin wooden stick in her right hand as if she just finished a small ritual or is about to start a dance. Her left arm hangs loosely by her side, slightly bent, giving a natural, unposed feel. She’s mid‑step, one foot forward, toes pointing slightly outward, with a subtle motion blur around her ankles that suggests movement.

- **Lighting & Exposure**  
  Warm golden hour light from the east casts soft highlights on her face and body, creating a slight overexposure in the upper left corner of the frame. The sun’s rays pierce through the tree canopy, producing gentle lens flare and a faint bokeh halo around the top of the tent. The exposure is slightly uneven—brighter at the top, darker at the bottom—to preserve a raw, unprocessed look.

- **Environment**  
  A tall green dome tent is set up behind her on a patch of dry pine needles and small rocks. The tent’s fabric shows natural creases and a slight sag at the roof, indicating it has been set for a few minutes. Tall pine trees line the background, their trunks forming a vertical rhythm that contrasts with the horizontal line of the tent. The ground is uneven, covered in fallen leaves and twigs, and a small stream of water runs along the right side of the frame, adding a subtle reflection.

- **Lens & Technical**  
  Use a wide‑angle lens (24–35 mm) with a short focal length to capture a broad view of the scene. Shoot in a single exposure that keeps everything—from the woman’s toes to the top of the tent—fully sharp, no portrait‑mode blur. The focus is planar, not depth‑of‑field‑based. Slight handheld shake is present, giving a natural motion freeze or slight blur around the edges. No rule‑of‑thirds intentionality—framing is accidental, slightly tilted, and off‑center.

- **Composition & Style**  
  The composition is candid and spontaneous: the woman’s body is slightly off‑center to the left, while the tent occupies the right side of the frame. The camera is held at a low angle, close to the ground, to emphasize the natural terrain. The shot has no post‑processing filters or color grading; it retains the raw, unprocessed look of a real‑world capture. A subtle tilt in the camera adds a dynamic, informal feel.
