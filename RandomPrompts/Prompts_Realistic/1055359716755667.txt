**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A young woman with wet, wind‑blown hair tied in a loose high ponytail, wearing an oversized beige cotton tee and distressed denim shorts. She’s mid‑step on a bustling city sidewalk, left arm extended as if she just tossed a coffee cup or grabbed a souvenir, right hand in a natural, relaxed position. Her face is turned slightly toward the camera, a candid smile with a hint of surprise, eyes bright, forehead slightly shimmering from moisture.

- **Motion & Focus**  
  Slight motion blur on the moving arm and hair, capturing the fleeting energy of the moment. Everything remains fully sharp—no portrait‑mode depth‑of‑field blur; background is crisp from a wide‑angle lens. The image has an auto‑HDR feel but no color grading or filters—just raw, unprocessed light.

- **Lighting & Exposure**  
  Natural daylight, mid‑afternoon sun casting soft shadows and a faint overexposed halo on the subject’s chest. Slight ambient glare from the street umbrellas and building facades. The exposure is slightly warm, with a subtle high‑key tint that gives the scene an energetic, spontaneous vibe.

- **Environment**  
  A busy downtown pedestrian street in an American city (e.g., New York or Los Angeles). Tall brick buildings line the avenue, a red “TGI” sign hangs above a café, a blue awning is visible on a neighboring shop. A few passersby are blurred in the background, one man in a black shirt walking left, another woman in a white tee walking right. An orange umbrella and a blue canopy add color contrast. The street is paved with concrete, a few scattered leaves and a small puddle reflect the sun.

- **Lens & Technical**  
  24‑mm wide‑angle lens (short focal length), handheld, slightly tilted. No bokeh—every element from foreground to background is in focus. The framing is accidental: the subject’s head is off‑center, the left edge of the frame cuts off a portion of her arm, creating a dynamic, unposed composition.

- **Style & Aesthetic**  
  Candid, spontaneous, “mid‑action” snapshot. Imperfections are welcome: slight tilt, partial crop, natural jitter. The image feels like a real-life photograph taken with a DSLR or mirrorless camera in daylight, captured on a bright afternoon. No studio lighting, no professional composition, no editorial tone—just an authentic, raw moment frozen in time.
