**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A young woman with dark, wavy hair standing outdoors in a casual, unposed stance. She’s wearing a black fishnet bodysuit that clings to her body, highlighting her curves and the tattoos on both arms and thighs—one a sunburst design on the upper left arm, another a rose on the left hip, and a third a sun‑face on the right thigh. Her hands rest on her hips, slightly angled, as if she’s just finished a gesture or is about to do a dance move. The pose has a slight tilt, giving an accidental, candid feel.

- **Lighting & Exposure**  
  Natural daylight from a mid‑morning sun, creating a warm, slightly overexposed highlight on her face and upper body. The lighting is uneven, with soft shadows cast by the foliage behind her. No auto‑HDR or beauty filters—just raw, unprocessed light that gives a genuine, snapshot vibe.

- **Environment**  
  The background is a rustic, semi‑urban setting: a green stucco house with a corrugated roof, a small wooden deck, and a cluster of spiky succulents and dry reeds behind her. The scene is bathed in bright, natural light with a slight haze from the ambient heat. The time of day is early afternoon, when the sun is high but not directly overhead, producing soft, warm tones.

- **Lens & Technical**  
  Use a wide‑angle lens (24–35 mm) with a short focal length to capture a broad view of the subject and environment. Shoot everything in focus—no bokeh or depth‑of‑field blur. The image is captured handheld, so there’s a subtle motion blur from slight camera shake and a minor off‑center framing that gives it a spontaneous, “in‑the‑moment” feel. All elements—the woman, the house, the plants—are fully sharp, as if the camera’s autofocus locked onto every detail.

- **Composition & Style**  
  The composition is intentionally imperfect: the frame is slightly tilted, the subject isn’t centered, and the crop cuts off part of her left hip, adding to the candid vibe. No rule‑of‑thirds is followed; instead, the framing feels accidental, as if the photographer was capturing a fleeting moment. The overall tone is naturalistic, with zero studio lighting or professional post‑processing—just a raw, realistic photograph that feels like a genuine snapshot from a casual outdoor session.
