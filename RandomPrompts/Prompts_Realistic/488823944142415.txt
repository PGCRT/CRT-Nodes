**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A young woman with long, straight brown hair, wearing a fitted, long‑sleeved gray henley that clings to her body. She’s mid‑step, slightly turned toward the camera, with one hand in her pocket and the other near her hip. Her face is partially obscured by a faint motion blur, as if she were just caught in an unplanned moment.

- **Capture Style**  
  Candid, unposed, spontaneous — subject unaware or mid‑action. Slight tilt and off‑center framing; partial crop that cuts off the top of her head and the lower part of her thighs. No rule‑of‑thirds intentionality — accidental framing. The image has a raw, unprocessed look: no filters, no color grading, no post‑processing aesthetic. A hint of overexposure in the center of the frame gives it an ambient, natural feel.

- **Environment**  
  Location: A concrete alleyway on a quiet suburban street. Time of day: Late afternoon, around 5:30 pm, with soft, warm sunlight casting long shadows. Weather: Light breeze, clear sky, a few scattered clouds. The background is a plain gray wall and a patch of green grass, fully sharp with no bokeh.

- **Lens & Technical**  
  Short focal length (24 mm) wide‑angle lens. Everything in focus — no portrait mode or subject isolation. Slight motion blur on the subject’s hands and hair, indicating handheld instability. No depth‑of‑field blur; background fully sharp. The exposure is slightly high, giving a bright center with a subtle halo around the subject.

- **Composition Notes**  
  Frame the shot so that the woman occupies roughly the central third of the frame, but leave a little space on the left side for a natural “mid‑action” feel. Keep the top of her head slightly cropped to add spontaneity. The bottom of the frame cuts off just above her knees, keeping the focus on her torso and upper legs.

- **Final Prompt**  
  "A young woman with long brown hair in a fitted gray henley, captured mid‑step in a concrete alleyway at late afternoon, with slight motion blur and accidental framing. The image is raw and unprocessed, slightly overexposed, shot on a 24 mm wide‑angle lens, everything in focus, background fully sharp, no bokeh."
