**Realistic Candid Photography Prompt Framework**

- **Subject & Pose**  
  A young woman with a high ponytail, wearing an olive‑green hooded sweatshirt, standing on a city sidewalk. Her back is to the camera, hands on hips, head turned slightly left as if she’s about to say something or react to a passing event. The hoodie’s drawstring is loose, the fabric softly drapes over her shoulders, and the cuffs are slightly bunched at the wrists.

- **Motion & Focus**  
  Slight motion blur on the hands and back of the sweatshirt, indicating a handheld shot captured mid‑movement. Everything in the frame—from the woman’s hair to the brick wall behind—is fully sharp; no portrait‑mode depth‑of‑field blur. The focus is intentional but casual, with no rule‑of‑thirds intentionality—framing is accidental and off‑center.

- **Lighting & Exposure**  
  Natural daylight, mid‑afternoon, with a slight overexposure on the upper part of the frame that gives a warm, raw feel. No auto‑HDR or auto‑beauty mode; no color grading or post‑processing. The exposure is uneven—slightly brighter at the top, creating a subtle halo around the subject’s head.

- **Lens & Technical**  
  Wide‑angle lens (24‑35 mm), short focal length. No bokeh; background is fully sharp. The shot uses a wide‑angle “handheld” feel with slight tilt and a partial crop that cuts off part of the sidewalk on the right side.

- **Environment**  
  A brick storefront with graffiti on the left, a concrete sidewalk stretching into the distance, and a blurred street scene in the background (other pedestrians, a passing bicycle). The time of day is late afternoon, with soft sunlight casting long shadows. Weather is clear, a light breeze is visible in the subject’s hair.

- **Composition & Style**  
  Candid, unposed, spontaneous—subject appears unaware of the camera. Slight tilt and off‑center framing create a casual, documentary feel. The composition is not intentionally rule‑of‑thirds; it’s a “mid‑action” snapshot that feels natural and raw.
