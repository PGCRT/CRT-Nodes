Dr. Lena Petrova, a brilliant but disgraced astrophysicist, stands in a remote desert observatory, having been kicked out of the scientific community for her radical theories. | She aligns her custom-built telescope to a patch of sky that is supposed to be completely empty. | Through the lens, she sees it: a faint, almost invisible crack in the fabric of space-time. As she watches, a single, massive, organic-looking eye blinks open on the other side. Her theories weren't just right; they were a terrifying understatement.
